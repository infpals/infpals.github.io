About Us

InfPALS is peer support for 1st year Informatics students.
As a group of 2nd and 3rd year students we will guide you through difficulties with course material and help you apply your knowledge in new situations to strengthen your understanding. Together we will ease your transition into your new-found university life!

What do we do?

Previous Next

Through discussing previous students' experiences we have developed student lead InfPALS sessions which are created to accommodate all of the students' educational and orientation needs.

These sessions are happening weekly lasting about an hour. These sessions usually start with an icebreaker then the main part of the sessions takes place. Main part of the session are activities/tasks which are reflecting topics covered in main computer science related degrees courses. After these activities/tasks students take a debreath, reflect on the session and freely ask any questions.

When and where the sessions happening?

Previous Next

There are usually 3 to 4 sessions each week happening on different days so students can choose which session they want to attend. The times of sessions have been adjusted to 1st years students courses so there should not be any clashes between the InfPALS sessions and live lectures or tutorials.

The sessions usually take place in the 5th floor of Appleton Tower room 507. The room where the sessions are happening can also be seen in the picture.
